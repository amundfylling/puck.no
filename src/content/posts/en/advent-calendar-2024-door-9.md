Behind door 8 we found Norway's best female table hockey player — none other than Anne Grethe Gangsøy. Many guessed Øyvind Rønne, who won the Norwegian Championship in 2006. As "the centre feint" pointed out, Øyvind Rønne was not necessarily the correct answer. On top of that, Anne Grethe did not compete in the open class at the 2007 World Championship in Moscow. So it was a tough door, and nobody got it right.

Luckily we're not even halfway to Christmas Eve yet, so plenty of new chances are coming! Who is hiding behind door 9?

**1. What is/was your favourite combination or trick?**

Sjøløve.

**2. What is your favourite lunch at a table hockey tournament?**

A meat patty between two slices of bread.

**3. Which tournament do/did you most enjoy travelling to?**

The Norwegian Championship will always rank highest. Great banquets, the thrashing handed out to Yngve, and the Puck Awards are among the highlights of the year.

**4. What is your best table hockey moment?**

It has to be either my first European Championship in Riga or the win at Lambertseter Open. I'll go with the former.

**5. When do you get up in the morning if the tournament starts at 10:00 and you have a 15-minute trip to the venue?**

08.30. That also leaves plenty of time to stop by a shop and pick up what you need (Red Bull, a banana and New Energy). Warming up is part of the fundamentals.
